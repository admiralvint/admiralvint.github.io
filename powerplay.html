conviction summary
BUY NOW
PEG, DUK, EXC
Cleanest balance sheets in the group. No wildfire exposure. Reasonable forward multiples (17–18.5x). Positive or near-positive FCF. EPS growth confirmed. PEG has the best risk/reward with nuclear upside optionality. DUK is the defensive anchor. EXC is the most undervalued clean story.
CAUTIOUS BUY
AEP, SRE
Genuine growth stories but valuation / balance sheet require discipline. AEP at 21x is expensive for a pipeline story that still needs to convert. SRE's 7–9% CAGR is exceptional but 89% payout + -$9.26 FCF/share means zero margin of safety if credit conditions tighten. Size conservatively — add on dips.
SPECULATIVE
EIX, XEL, PCG
Maximum 5% per position. All carry binary wildfire or regulatory risk. PCG cheapest at 10x but two prior bankruptcies. EIX at 11x has 40–80% upside IF wildfire resolves. XEL has strongest analyst consensus and Google deal. Do not enter EIX or PCG before November — CA/CO fire season active June–October.
WAIT FOR DIP
NEE, SO
Both are excellent businesses run by excellent management teams. Neither is worth buying today. NEE at 24x after +44% in 52 weeks with $94B in debt and no FCF has essentially no margin of safety. SO at 22x with Hold consensus and target flat to current is priced for perfection. Target: NEE at $75–$80, SO at $82–$85.
TSLA ENERGY
Not a utility play
Tesla Energy (13% of TSLA revenue, 23% of gross profit in H1 2025) cannot be purchased separately. You buy the entire company including Automotive (75% rev), which is under significant competitive and brand pressure. TSLA is a bet on Elon Musk, FSD, and robotics — not a utility infrastructure investment.
SECTOR WARNING
All 10 are correlated
Every stock in this list benefits from the same macro thesis (AI power demand). In a recession, AI capex pull-back, or sustained rate rise cycle, all 10 get hurt simultaneously. 7 of 10 have negative FCF. Diversifying across all 10 does not reduce the macro risk — it concentrates it. Size the total sector allocation with that in mind.
